Internet basics (in 2 minutes)

Addresses (DNS)

DNS is the internet’s address book. When you type your website name, DNS tells browsers where to go.

More

A records point to a server’s street address (an IP). MX records tell email where to deliver. TXT records carry notes like SPF and DMARC policies that help stop email spoofing.

Locks and keys (HTTPS)

HTTPS is the “lock” icon. It encrypts traffic so others can’t read or change it in transit.

More

TLS is the modern lock. We check the lock is current and not expiring soon. Latest is TLS 1.3.

Doors (Ports)

Ports are doors into your system. For public websites, usually only 80 (web) and 443 (secure web) should be open.

More

We quickly test common doors to confirm only the ones you expect are available.

What to fix first

  1. Add a Permissions‑Policy to limit powerful features (camera, mic, etc.).
  2. Tighten your Content‑Security‑Policy (avoid wildcards and “unsafe‑inline”).
  3. Enhance HSTS (includeSubDomains + preload) if it fits your rollout plan.
  4. Move DMARC to “reject” once confident, so spoofed emails are blocked not just flagged.
  5. Review any flagged takeover — remove unused DNS or publish content with the provider.
Why it matters: These steps reduce phishing risk, prevent common web attacks, and improve trust.

Email safety 101 (SPF & DMARC)

Goal: Make it hard for scammers to send fake emails that look like they’re from you.

  • SPF: a list of who’s allowed to send on your behalf. If it’s not on the list, it should be rejected.
  • DMARC: a policy that tells mail providers what to do with suspicious emails (monitor, quarantine, reject).
Recommended path: Start with “quarantine” to monitor, then move to “reject” once you’re confident all legitimate senders are covered.

FAQ

Will these changes break my site?

Changes like CSP and HSTS should be tested in staging first. Start strict, then loosen carefully if needed.

How often should we run this?

Monthly is a good cadence, plus after major launches or DNS changes.

We use a CDN — does that help?

Yes. CDNs often improve security and performance. This report still checks your visible setup.

Keep it green

  • Re‑run this checkup regularly or after big changes.
  • Document who owns each subdomain and app.
  • Review email deliverability when tightening SPF/DMARC.
